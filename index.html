Rissois de Galinha

Crispy fried pastries filled with a creamy, savory chicken filling.

Ingredients:

  • For the dough: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 cup water, 2 tbsp butter, a pinch of salt.
  • For the filling: 1 lb boneless chicken breast, 1 onion, 2 cloves garlic, 1 cup milk, 2 tbsp flour, olive oil, salt and pepper.

Instructions:

  1. To make the dough, boil water, butter, and salt in a pot. Add flour all at once and stir vigorously until a dough ball forms. Let it cool.
  2. For the filling, boil the chicken until cooked, then shred it.
  3. In a separate pan, sauté chopped onion and garlic in olive oil. Add the flour and stir. Slowly whisk in the milk to create a thick béchamel sauce.
  4. Stir the shredded chicken into the sauce, and season with salt and pepper. Let the filling cool completely.
  5. Roll out the dough into a thin sheet. Cut out circles using a round cookie cutter or glass. Place a spoonful of the chicken filling on one half of each circle.
  6. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape, and press the edges with a fork to seal.
  7. In a separate bowl, prepare an egg wash. Dip each pastry into the egg wash, then coat it with breadcrumbs.
  8. Deep-fry the patties in hot oil until they are golden brown and crispy.

Rissois de Camarão

A classic Portuguese snack, these are delicious deep-fried pastries with a creamy shrimp filling.

Ingredients:

  • For the dough: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 cup water, 2 tbsp butter, a pinch of salt.
  • For the filling: 1 lb small shrimp, 1 onion, 2 cloves garlic, 1 cup milk, 2 tbsp flour, olive oil, chopped fresh parsley, salt and pepper.

Instructions:

  1. Make the dough following the same instructions as the chicken patties. Let it cool.
  2. For the filling, sauté chopped onion and garlic in olive oil. Add shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes. Remove the shrimp and chop them into small pieces.
  3. In the same pan, add the flour and stir. Slowly add the milk, whisking until you have a smooth sauce.
  4. Return the chopped shrimp to the pan. Add the chopped parsley, and season with salt and pepper. Let the filling cool completely.
  5. Roll out the dough and cut out circles. Place a spoonful of the shrimp filling on one half of each circle.
  6. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon, and press the edges with a fork to seal.
  7. Dip each pastry in egg wash and then coat it with breadcrumbs.
  8. Deep-fry the patties in hot oil until they are golden brown and crispy.

Caldo Verde

A popular Portuguese soup with a deep green color, made with potatoes, kale, and sausage.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 bunch collard greens (or kale), thinly shredded
  • 1 lb chouriço sausage, sliced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 6 cups water or broth

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté onion and garlic in olive oil until soft.
  2. Add the diced potatoes and water/broth. Bring to a boil, then simmer until potatoes are very soft.
  3. Mash the potatoes directly in the pot to thicken the soup.
  4. Add the sliced chouriço and simmer for a few minutes.
  5. Stir in the shredded collard greens and cook until they are tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with a drizzle of olive oil.

Porco à Alentejana

A flavorful dish from the Alentejo region, combining marinated pork, clams, and potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork loin, cut into cubes
  • 1 lb fresh clams, rinsed
  • 1 lb potatoes, cubed and fried
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh coriander
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the pork cubes for at least 4 hours (or overnight) in a mixture of paprika, garlic, white wine, bay leaf, salt, and pepper.
  2. In a large pan, sear the marinated pork until browned on all sides.
  3. Add the clams and cover the pan. Cook until the clams open.
  4. Remove the pork and clams. In the same pan, add the fried potatoes to soak up the juices.
  5. Return the pork and clams to the pan. Sprinkle with fresh coriander and serve hot.

Bacalhau à Brás

A traditional dish made from shreds of salted cod, onions, and thinly chopped fried potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb salted cod (bacalhau), desalted
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 lbs potatoes, thinly chopped into matchsticks and fried
  • 6 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1/2 cup black olives
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Soak the salted cod for 24-48 hours, changing the water frequently. Boil, then shred the cod.
  2. In a pan, sauté the sliced onion in olive oil until soft. Add the shredded cod and cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Mix in the fried potato sticks.
  4. Pour the beaten eggs over the mixture, stirring gently until the eggs are just set and creamy.
  5. Garnish with black olives and fresh parsley before serving.

Feijoada à Transmontana

A rich and hearty bean stew with various cuts of pork, from the Trás-os-Montes region.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb dry white beans, soaked overnight
  • 1 lb pork belly, cubed
  • 1 lb pork sausage (chouriço), sliced
  • 1 lb smoked pork ear or foot
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Olive oil, salt, and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked beans. In a large pot, add the beans, pork ear/foot, and enough water to cover. Simmer until the pork is tender.
  2. In a separate skillet, sauté the onion and garlic in olive oil until soft. Add the pork belly and sausage, cooking until browned.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes and bay leaves to the skillet. Cook for a few minutes.
  4. Add the contents of the skillet to the pot with the beans and pork. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Simmer the stew for at least 30-40 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Serve hot.

Pastéis de Nata

A Portuguese egg tart pastry, often dusted with cinnamon.

Ingredients:

  • 1 roll puff pastry
  • 6 egg yolks
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 strip of lemon peel

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 550°F (290°C). Roll out puff pastry and press into muffin tins.
  2. In a saucepan, whisk together egg yolks, sugar, milk, and flour.
  3. Add the cinnamon stick and lemon peel. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens.
  4. Remove cinnamon and lemon peel. Pour the custard into the pastry-lined tins, filling them 3/4 full.
  5. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the tops are beautifully charred and the pastry is golden.

Arroz Doce

A classic Portuguese rice pudding, creamy and rich, often topped with cinnamon.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup short-grain rice
  • 1 liter (about 4 cups) milk
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 1 lemon peel
  • 4 egg yolks
  • Pinch of salt
  • Ground cinnamon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice. In a large pot, combine the rice with 2 cups of the milk, cinnamon sticks, and lemon peel.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, stirring frequently, until the rice is tender and has absorbed most of the liquid (about 20-25 minutes).
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the sugar and a pinch of salt. Slowly add the remaining milk to the egg mixture, whisking constantly.
  4. Remove the cinnamon sticks and lemon peel from the rice. Gradually pour the egg and milk mixture into the rice pot, stirring constantly over low heat.
  5. Continue to cook for about 5-10 minutes, stirring constantly, until the pudding thickens. Do not let it boil.
  6. Remove from heat and pour into a large serving dish or individual bowls. Let it cool, then refrigerate.
  7. Before serving, sprinkle generously with ground cinnamon.

Bolo de Bolacha

A famous no-bake Portuguese cake made with coffee-soaked cookies and a rich cream filling.

Ingredients:

  • 2 packages (about 400g) Maria cookies
  • 1 cup brewed coffee, cooled
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1/2 tsp instant coffee powder

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar until the mixture is light and fluffy.
  2. Gradually add the egg yolks, one by one, beating well after each addition. Mix in the instant coffee powder.
  3. Dip a Maria cookie into the cool coffee for just a second, then place it on a serving plate.
  4. Spread a thin layer of the cream mixture over the cookie. Repeat this process, stacking the cookies and cream in a circular shape to form the cake.
  5. Use the remaining cream to cover the entire cake.
  6. Refrigerate for at least 6 hours (or overnight) to allow the cookies to soften and the cake to set. Serve chilled.
